<p><p>Corrosion inhibitors for the oil and gas market are substances that prevent or reduce corrosion in pipelines, tanks, and processing equipment. They are primarily categorized into two types: organic and inorganic. Organic inhibitors, derived from carbon-containing compounds, often provide effective protection through film formation and can be tailored for specific environments. Inorganic inhibitors, typically composed of metals or mineral compounds, function by altering the electrochemical processes on metallic surfaces. Each type has distinct advantages depending on the application and environmental conditions.</p></p>
