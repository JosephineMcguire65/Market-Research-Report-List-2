<p><p>The dyes market is categorized into several types based on their application and chemical properties. Disperse dyes are primarily used for synthetic fibers, notably polyester. Reactive dyes chemically bond with fibers, making them ideal for cotton and other cellulosic materials. Sulfur dyes are known for their excellent lightfastness, mainly used for denim. Vat dyes are water-insoluble and require reduction for application, suitable for cotton. Acid dyes are used for protein fibers like wool and silk. Other categories include natural dyes and specialty formulations for niche applications.</p></p>
